The Journey of a Diamond

Home · Journey
Step by Step

From Deep Earth to Brilliant Light

Click each step to read more.

STEP 01

Mining

Diamonds form 150 km below Earth's surface, brought up by ancient kimberlite pipes.

At Jwaneng and Orapa, we operate massive open-pit mines. Trucks the size of houses haul kimberlite ore to processing plants where rough diamonds are recovered.
STEP 02

Processing

Crushing, scrubbing, and dense-medium separation extract every carat.

X-ray luminescence and laser sorting separate diamonds from waste rock with extraordinary precision — even microscopic diamonds are recovered.
STEP 03

Sorting

At Diamond Trading Company Botswana — the world's largest sort house.

Each rough diamond is graded on the 4Cs (Carat, Color, Clarity, Cut potential) — over 12,000 categories. AI-assisted scanners now classify millions of carats per year.
STEP 04

Cutting & Polishing

Master craftsmen turn rough stones into 57+ perfect facets.

Cutting can take days for a single stone. Botswana's growing cutting industry has created thousands of skilled jobs and added local value to every carat.
STEP 05

Export

Distributed worldwide through De Beers Sightholder Sales and Okavango Diamond Company.

Botswana now sells more rough diamonds than any other country. From Gaborone, polished gems travel to Antwerp, Mumbai, Tel Aviv, New York and beyond.
STEP 06

Retail

The final stone reaches collectors, jewelers, and lovers — a forever symbol.

Every diamond from Debswana is conflict-free, fully traceable, and represents the hands of thousands of Batswana who made it possible.
